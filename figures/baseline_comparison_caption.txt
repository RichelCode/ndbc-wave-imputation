Mean absolute error (m/s, log scale) of single-variable WSPD imputation on the 2022-2023 test split of 27 offshore NDBC Gulf of Mexico buoys. Bars show mean and ±1 standard deviation across the three seeds within each mask family. Methods compared: GRIN, linear, LOCF, climatology, k-NN. Mask families: MCAR at three rates (10%, 25%, 50%), Block MCAR (72-hour blocks, 25% rate), and Empirical MNAR (25% rate, 80% of hidden cells concentrated in storm windows). The dashed line at MAE = 5.54 m/s marks the naive zero-prediction floor (mean|truth| on the test split). Key empirical observation: the optimal method depends on the missingness scope. Temporal interpolation methods (linear, LOCF) dominate when hidden cells have nearby observed neighbors in time (MCAR); the graph-aware GRIN dominates on Block MCAR (72-hour gaps) where temporal neighbors are unavailable but spatial ones remain informative. No single method wins across all families; all methods sit between the climatology baseline and the zeros floor.
